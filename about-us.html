Skilled Nursing Facilities

Affiliated Dialysis California provides bedside hemodialysis in skilled nursing facilities. Our new program is the first and only Office of Statewide Health Planning and Development (OSHPD) and California Department of Public Health (CDPH) licensed provider of bedside dialysis in skilled nursing facilities in California. ADC of California works in conjunction with skilled nursing facilities to support them in acquiring OSHPD and CDPH certification. ADC provides a complete solution that includes; personnel, project management, program oversight, interdisciplinary care plans and equipment. In addition, ADC can provide managed care contracting support. We support skilled nursing facility dialysis because it is good for both patients and their care providers. Our staff can provide bedside dialysis for both ventilator dependent and your standard SNF patients. Benefits include superior patient outcomes and elimination of transportation complexities. Transport to outside dialysis centers causes patients to miss medications, meals and therapy sessions. The comfort of being treated at the bedside in a skilled nursing facility is accompanied by improved patient survival. Clinical data has shown patient mortality rates drop by 70% when transportation is eliminated.

ADC is the leader in providing comprehensive solutions for bedside dialysis in skilled nursing facilities.

What we offer

Complex dialysis patients:

Patients who have end stage kidney failure and additional complex conditions can be difficult to place in outpatient dialysis facilities. Examples include ventilator dependent patients and cardiac patients with continuous mechanical or chemical support needs. These complex patients often get trapped in acute care hospitals where they may stay many months or even years because very few outpatient dialysis facilities will accept these types of patients. When there is the support necessary in their homes, some complex patients can get out of medical facilities and return home with ADC providing the dialysis support. We have a long track record of bringing patients home who were previously considered non dischargeable patients. ADC provides assistance in coordinating care with other disciplines and caregivers of all levels. Our physicians do home visits. Most cases of this nature require a dialysis nurse or licensed patient care technician be provided by ADC to perform the dialysis treatment. When appropriate, family members may be trained to be the Dialysis Care Giver.

When patients do not have adequate support in their home setting the other alternative becomes bedside Dialysis in a skilled nursing facility where ADC is contracted to provide dialysis support.

Nocturnal Home Hemodialysis:

This type of dialysis is performed 6 to 8 hours at night, Usually every other night or up to five nights per week while the patient is sleeping. Nocturnal home dialysis is more like normal kidney function because it is done for longer periods of time as well as more often than conventional in center three times a week dialysis. It is the form of hemodialysis that delivers the greatest toxin and fluid removal. All of the benefits of More Frequent Dialysis are gained but even more so as the dose of dialysis increases greatly with nocturnal dialysis.

An alternative form of dialysis is Peritoneal Dialysis -also called PD.

PD is home dialysis treatment that uses the patients peritoneum (which is the inner lining of your abdomen) as a filter to exchange fluid and dissolved substances and toxins from the blood. In this case fluid is introduced through a catheter or a tube that's permanently placed in the abdomen. The newly instilled clean fluid absorbs the toxins from the body and then is removed and new clean fluid is instilled into the abdomen again. When this is done manually we call that continuous ambulatory peritoneal dialysis or CAPD. A machine or a cycler can also do it at night while the patient is sleeping, referred to as Automated Peritoneal Dialysis.
